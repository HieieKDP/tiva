TIVA

Temporal Integrity and Validation Architecture

Execution governance for Physical AI under constrained compute.

Prithvi Bhat · prithvibhat19@gmail.com

Executive summary

Physical AI systems do not fail only when models are wrong. They fail when limited compute, competing tasks, and deadlines force the stack to keep acting on outdated perception while health checks still look nominal.

For teams deploying robots and autonomous systems on constrained edge hardware, TIVA asks a direct question: is this output still trustworthy to act on, right now, under load?

TIVA is not another inference framework. It is temporal execution governance at the perception→action boundary: what may enter inference, and what may influence motion, with auditable traces as the substrate.

Today (Stage 1): one perception→action loop; temporal observations (JSONL), offline KPI, governed vs ungoverned replay under stress.
Direction (Stages 2–4): multi-stream, criticality-aware governance on shared edge compute, toward a predictive execution layer for Physical AI.

The constraint: Physical AI is not one model

Most AI stacks assume abundant compute and optimize individual models. Deployed robots face a different problem:

  • Multiple cameras and sensors
  • Planning, localization, mapping
  • Safety monitoring and human interaction
  • Often one GPU/NPU/CPU budget on the edge

The challenge is allocating finite compute so the system maximizes mission success while respecting safety constraints, especially when overloaded.

Under contention, the failure mode is often freshness: perception falls behind reality, but motion continues. The system appears healthy by traditional metrics, yet acts on stale state.

The execution governor

The layer we are building toward answers system-level questions, not model-level ones:

  • Which model or stream should run next?
  • Which inference can be delayed or skipped?
  • Which perception task must not miss its effective deadline?
  • When should a lower-fidelity path be used?
  • How should compute be redistributed after a fault or spike?
  • What happens when the system is overloaded, gracefully, not catastrophically?

This is runtime governance, not a single scheduler knob: an execution layer Physical AI stacks could depend on, not a point solution for one workload.

Perception models are unchanged. No retraining. TIVA sits beside existing models and controllers.

Substrate: a temporal observation contract in runtime traces (capture → infer → emit) and offline KPI (age-of-information, inference delay, emit cadence). Governance decisions are tied to replayable evidence, not opaque tuning.

Why this category now

Physical AI is moving from demos to always-on deployment. The gap is increasingly execution and deployment, not model capability alone:

  • The last mile between training/simulation and reliable field operation
  • A missing execution layer once agents and robots run continuously at scale
  • Timing and freshness failures that surface only after a robot misbehaves under load

TIVA targets that layer: criticality-aware execution governance under constrained compute, with observability built in from Stage 1.
